Capital City of Tajikistan

Dushanbe is the capital and largest city of Tajikistan. Located in the Hisor Valley, it's known for its wide tree-lined avenues, neoclassical buildings, and vibrant bazaars.

Essential Travel Information

Everything you need to know to plan your perfect Tajikistan adventure.

Transportation

Air Travel

International flights arrive at Dushanbe International Airport, with connections from major hubs in Russia, Turkey, and UAE.

Local Transport

Shared taxis, marshrutkas (minibuses), and private car rentals are common options for getting around cities and between destinations.

Road Conditions

Major highways are well-maintained, but mountain roads may require 4WD vehicles, especially in remote areas and during winter.

Learn More

Accommodation

Hotels

Modern hotels are available in major cities like Dushanbe and Khujand, ranging from luxury to budget options.

Homestays

Experience authentic Tajik hospitality by staying with local families, especially popular in rural areas and along trekking routes.

Camping

Camping is popular in natural areas, with designated sites available in national parks and along trekking routes.

Learn More

Visa Information

Requirements

Most visitors need a visa to enter Tajikistan. Check the latest requirements based on your nationality before traveling.

E-Visa

Apply for an e-visa online through the official government portal. Processing typically takes 3-5 business days.

GBAO Permit

A special permit is required for visiting the Gorno-Badakhshan Autonomous Region, which includes the Pamir Mountains.

Local Customs

Greetings

Handshakes are common, though men may place their hand over their heart when greeting women. "Salom" is the standard greeting.

Dress Code

Modest dress is appreciated, especially in rural areas. Cover shoulders and knees, particularly when visiting religious sites.

Dining Etiquette

Remove shoes when entering homes, accept tea when offered, and it's polite to try all dishes served by your host.
